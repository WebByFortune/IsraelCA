Our History

Hostory of the Church Routes around Janeno Jackson Filmona Orwa and Jadolo David Zakayo Kivuli. The two had earlier met in Kabete Where they both trained. It is here that Kivuli mentored Janeno in matters religion and evangelism.
in 1941, The holy spirit descended on Janeno Jackson Filmona Orwa. He formed Jo Roho Church after leaving CMS.He officially opened the church in Nyahera- a function attended by Jadolo Kivuli. Janeno Filmona was hardworking, polite, patient, kind, obedient, understanding and hospitable.
In 1942 Jadolo Kivuli broke away from PAOC/PAG and the two leaders agreed to mearge since they both had common beliefs in baptism by holy spirit, visions, dreams and prophesies
The first proposed name for the Church wa Huru salvation, which was not accepted by the government due to the nationwide agitation for independence at the time. They settled for African Israel Church Nineveh.The name Israel was a revelation to J.F. Orwa by the holy spirit that David Kivuli was the vessel for Israel.
The Church enjoyed Relative peace and growth under David Kivuli as the High Priest and J.F. Orwa as the Deputy due to their devine and charismatic personalities.
The first church constitution was made in 1957.
In 1974, David Kivuli passes on. This led to disunity in the church. Later in 1978 the disunity escalated after some church leaders were excommunicated.
In 1979, Israel Church of Africa was formed by the excommunicated members led by J.F. Orwa. They agreed to worship as Africa Israel Church Diocese of Nyanza. The church faced threats from the Nineveh headquaters including the closure of the church and unlawful detentions.
In 1987 The church was deregistered. The following year (1988) a caretaker committee was constituted to spearhead the formation of Israel Church of Africa under the assistance of Dr. Robert Ouko.
In September 15th 1989, Israel Church Of Africa was officially registered. It was agreed that dioceses will be oppened in the administrative provinces.
